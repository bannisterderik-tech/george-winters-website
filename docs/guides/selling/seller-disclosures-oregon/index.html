Selling · Field guide

Seller disclosure in Oregon: honesty as strategy

The short answer

Oregon requires sellers to complete statutory property disclosures for buyers, covering the property’s condition and history as known. Beyond compliance, disclosure done thoroughly is strategy: transparent files build the buyer confidence premium properties trade on, while discovered concealment poisons transactions and follows sellers legally. When in doubt, disclose.

How the obligation works

The statutory form asks what you know across the property’s systems and history; buyers receive it with revocation rights tied to its delivery. It asks for your knowledge honestly stated — not omniscience, not warranty — and “unknown” is an honest answer where true.

Rural properties add texture: wells, septic, water rights, easements, fire history, and outbuilding permits all have disclosure surface. The guides across this site map the substance; the form asks what you know of it.

Candor as advantage

Educated buyers cross-check everything; discovered gaps reprice deals brutally and revive exit rights at the worst moments. Complete disclosure inverts the dynamic — questions answered before asked, inspection findings pre-explained, trust compounding toward closing.

Pair disclosure with documentation (the records file) and disclosure stops being defensive paperwork entirely: it becomes the property’s well-organized biography, doing sales work.

Local rule of thumb: Disclose like the buyer will discover everything anyway — because the good ones do.
FAQ

Related questions

What if I genuinely do not know?

The form accommodates honest unknowns — distinguishing them from knowledge is the integrity line. Where unknowns are consequential, pre-listing inspection converts them profitably into knowledge.

Do as-is sales skip disclosure?

“As-is” addresses repair expectations, not honesty obligations — statutory disclosure duties and fraud principles persist. Verify specifics with your transaction professionals.

How does fire history disclose?

Factually and plainly, like the valley handles it generally — what occurred, what was remediated or rebuilt, with documentation. See the fire-affected land guide for the diligence mirror-image.

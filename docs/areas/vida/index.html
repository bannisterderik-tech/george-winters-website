The postcard of the corridor

Vida, Oregon

Vida is the valley’s postcard — home of the Goodpasture covered bridge and classic mid-corridor river living.

Vida owns the corridor's most famous view. The Goodpasture covered bridge — white, wooden, and photographed constantly since 1938 — crosses the McKenzie here, and everything about the community lives up to the postcard: emerald water, timbered hills, and river properties people dream about.

The 2020 fire touched parts of this stretch, and Vida's story since has been one of rebuilding with grit — newer construction now stands alongside classic cabins, and the community's spirit is stronger than ever.

Mid-corridor position gives Vida a balanced life: about half an hour to Springfield, with the upper valley's recreation — reservoirs, hot springs, trail country — just up the road.

Vida at a glance

  • Goodpasture covered bridge (1938) — among Oregon’s most photographed
  • Classic mid-corridor river community, roughly 30 minutes from Springfield
  • Mix of longtime cabins and newer post-2020 construction
  • Gateway position between the lower valley and the upper river

FAQ

Vida questions, answered

What is the Goodpasture Bridge?

A 1938 white covered bridge spanning the McKenzie at Vida — one of Oregon’s longest and most photographed covered bridges, and the enduring symbol of the corridor.

How far is Vida from Springfield and Eugene?

Roughly thirty minutes to Springfield services on Highway 126, and about forty to central Eugene, depending on season and traffic.

How did the 2020 fire affect Vida?

Parts of the Vida–Blue River stretch were affected, and the years since have brought significant rebuilding — newer homes now mix with the valley’s classic cabins. Property-specific history is part of normal due diligence here, handled respectfully and factually.

Is Vida riverfront expensive?

River frontage anywhere on the McKenzie carries a premium, and iconic mid-corridor stretches like Vida hold value well. Exact pricing depends on frontage, bank, systems, and improvements — I price by walking the property, not by algorithm.

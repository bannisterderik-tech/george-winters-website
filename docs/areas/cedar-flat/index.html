First taste of the valley

Cedar Flat, Oregon

Cedar Flat is where the corridor stops feeling like town — the first rural stretch past Thurston, with the river suddenly out the window.

Just past the last Thurston stoplight, the valley starts working on you. Cedar Flat is the corridor's first true rural stretch — small acreages, garden ground, and homes tucked between Highway 126 and the hills, with the McKenzie beginning to show itself through the trees.

This is transition country in the best sense. You are close enough to Springfield that a forgotten grocery item is a fifteen-minute round trip, yet the properties live rural: wells, septic systems, shops and outbuildings, room for animals and projects.

Cedar Flat rarely gets the headlines that Vida's covered bridge or Blue River's lakes get, and residents like it that way. It is the quiet, practical way to own a piece of the valley without giving up town.

FAQ

Cedar Flat questions, answered

Where is Cedar Flat on the corridor?

Cedar Flat is the first rural area east of Springfield’s Thurston district on Highway 126, before you reach Walterville. It marks the start of well-and-septic country on the McKenzie river road.

Are Cedar Flat properties on wells and septic?

Generally yes — most properties here run private wells and septic systems. Budget for well flow and water quality testing plus a septic inspection in any purchase.

How far is Cedar Flat from shopping and services?

Roughly ten to fifteen minutes to full services in Thurston and Springfield — close enough that many residents barely notice they have left town, which is exactly the appeal.

Is Cedar Flat a good fit for hobby farms?

Many properties suit gardens, small livestock, and shop projects. Confirm zoning and any land-use rules for the specific parcel before you plan — allowed uses vary by zone.
